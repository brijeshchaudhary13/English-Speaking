The **chest is one muscle group**, but it has **different regions/fibers** that we target from different angles.

Main chest muscles:

1. **Upper Chest**

   * Top area near collarbone
   * Best exercises:

     * Incline Dumbbell Press
     * Incline Bench Press

2. **Middle Chest**

   * Main central chest mass
   * Best exercises:

     * Flat Bench Press
     * Push-ups

3. **Lower Chest**

   * Bottom chest area
   * Best exercises:

     * Decline Press
     * Chest Dips

There’s also a deeper muscle under the chest:

* Pectoralis Major — the main large chest muscle
* Minor supporting muscle: **Pectoralis Minor** underneath

So on your **Chest Day**, we train from different angles:

* Flat movement → middle chest
* Incline movement → upper chest
* Fly/stretch movement → overall chest shaping

That’s why **3 chest exercises** are usually enough for a beginner:

1. Bench Press
2. Incline Dumbbell Press
3. Chest Fly

Trainer tip: Many beginners do only flat bench and wonder why the upper chest stays flat. Angles matter. 🔥

# Trainer golden rule

**Warm-up order:**

1. Increase body temperature
2. Mobilize joints
3. Activate muscles
4. Start first exercise with light weight

Example:
If your bench press working weight is **20 kg**, do:

* Empty bar ×15
* 10 kg ×10
* Then working sets

Warm-up prepares the body; it should **not make you tired**. 🔥
