# Singular Pronoun

These pronouns are treated as **singular** and usually take singular verbs like **is, does, has**.

| Singular Pronouns |
| ----------------- |
| One               |
| Each              |
| Every             |
| Everyone          |
| Everybody         |
| Everything        |
| Someone           |
| Somebody          |
| Something         |
| Anybody           |
| Anyone            |
| Anything          |
| Nobody            |
| Nothing           |

### Examples

* Everyone **is** ready.
* Somebody **has** a pen.
* Each student **does** the work.

# Singular Pronouns – Detailed Notes

---

# 1. One → एक / हर कोई (हर किसी को)

**Meaning:** Used for any person in a general way.

### Structure

* One + singular verb
* Uses **is / has / does**

### Examples

* One is here, one is in the kitchen.
* One student is weeping in the class.
* One should respect every person.
* One needs love more than money.

---

# 2. Each → दो या दो से ज्यादा में से हर एक

**Meaning:** Talks about every single person or thing separately.

### Rule

* Each + singular noun + singular verb

### Examples

* Each boy is my friend here.
* Each biscuit has chocolate.
* Each student does homework daily.
* Each player was ready.

---

# 3. Every → दो से ज्यादा में से हर एक

**Meaning:** Refers to all members of a group one by one.

### Rule

* Every + singular noun + singular verb

### Examples

* Every boy is my friend here.
* Every biscuit has chocolate.
* Every student was present.
* Every child likes toys.

---

# 4. Everyone / Everybody → हर कोई

**Meaning:** Refers to all people.

### Rule

* Everyone / Everybody + singular verb

### Examples

* Everybody is sleeping in the hall.
* Everyone was dancing except you.
* Everybody has a mobile phone.
* Everyone likes music.

---

# 5. Everything → हर चीज़

**Meaning:** Refers to all things.

### Rule

* Everything + singular verb

### Examples

* Everything here is beautiful.
* Everything you have is mine.
* Everything was ready.
* Everything looks perfect.

---

# Important Grammar Rule

All these pronouns are **Singular Pronouns**, so they take:

* **Is**
* **Was**
* **Has**
* **Does**

### Examples

* Everyone **is** happy.
* Each student **has** a book.
* Every player **does** practice.
* Everything **was** clean.

---

# Singular Pronouns – More Detailed Notes

---

# 6. Someone / Somebody → कोई

**Meaning:** Refers to an unknown person.

### Rule

* Someone / Somebody + singular verb

### Examples

* Someone is at the door.
* I have someone who helps me.
* Somebody has called you.
* Someone was waiting outside.

---

# 7. Anyone / Anybody → कोई

**Meaning:** Used mainly in questions and negative sentences.

### Rule

* Anyone / Anybody + singular verb

### Examples

* Is anyone at the door?
* I don’t have anyone who helps me.
* Anybody is allowed to join.
* Did anyone call me?

---

# 8. Something → कुछ

**Meaning:** Refers to an unknown thing.

### Rule

* Something + singular verb

### Examples

* Something is missing here.
* I have something to eat.
* Something was wrong.
* Something has changed.

---

# 9. Anything → कुछ

**Meaning:** Used mainly in questions and negative sentences for things.

### Rule

* Anything + singular verb

### Examples

* Is anything missing here?
* I don’t have anything to eat.
* Did you buy anything?
* Anything is possible.

---

# 10. Nothing → कुछ नहीं

**Meaning:** Means no thing.

### Rule

* Nothing + singular verb

### Examples

* Nothing is boring here.
* I have nothing to eat.
* Nothing was ready.
* Nothing has happened.

---

# Important Rule

All these are **Singular Pronouns**, therefore they use:

* **Is**
* **Was**
* **Has**
* **Does**

### Examples

* Someone **is** calling you.
* Nothing **was** there.
* Anybody **has** the right to speak.
* Something **does** smell bad.

# Plural Pronouns – Detailed Notes

---

# 1. Both → दोनों

**Meaning:** Refers to two persons or things together.

### Rule

* Both + plural verb

### Examples

* Both have cars.
* Both of you are good friends.
* Both were present in class.
* Both players are strong.

---

# 2. Few → कुछ

**Meaning:** Refers to a small number of people or things.

### Rule

* Few + plural noun + plural verb

### Examples

* Few are waiting for the train.
* Few girls were talking in the park.
* Few students have completed the task.
* Few people know the truth.

---

# 3. Many → कई

**Meaning:** Refers to a large number.

### Rule

* Many + plural noun + plural verb

### Examples

* Many are waiting for the train.
* Many girls were talking in the park.
* Many students have laptops.
* Many people like cricket.

---

# 4. Others → दूसरे

**Meaning:** Refers to remaining people or things.

### Rule

* Others + plural verb

### Examples

* Others have no idea about this matter.
* He is saying yes; others are saying no.
* Some students worked hard; others played.
* Others were absent yesterday.

---

# Important Rule

All these are **Plural Pronouns**, therefore they use:

* **Are**
* **Were**
* **Have**
* **Do**

### Examples

* Both **are** ready.
* Few **were** absent.
* Many **have** mobile phones.
* Others **do** the work properly.

---

# Comparison of Quantity

## Few < Several < Many < Numerous

| Word     | Meaning           |
| -------- | ----------------- |
| Few      | Small number      |
| Several  | More than few     |
| Many     | Large number      |
| Numerous | Very large number |

### Examples

* I have visited many cities in my life.
* Numerous people attended my brother’s wedding.
* Several students passed the exam.
* Few workers were available.

# Singular & Plural Pronouns – Detailed Notes

These pronouns can be used as **Singular** or **Plural** depending on the sentence.

---

# 1. All → सब / सभी

### Rule

* Use **singular verb** when “All” refers to one thing or uncountable noun.
* Use **plural verb** when “All” refers to many people or things.

---

## Singular Use of All

### Examples

* All is well.
* All is mine only.
* All was ready.
* All has changed.

---

## Plural Use of All

### Examples

* All are well.
* All are waiting for you.
* All were happy.
* All have completed the work.

---

# 2. Some → कुछ / थोड़ा

### Rule

* Use **singular verb** for uncountable nouns.
* Use **plural verb** for countable plural nouns.

---

## Plural Use of Some

### Examples

* Some people are coming.
* Some are dancing and some are sitting.
* Some students were absent.
* Some players have arrived.

---

## Singular Use of Some

### Examples

* Some water is required.
* Some money is saved for my study.
* Some milk was left.
* Some information is useful.

---

# 3. None → कोई नहीं

### Rule

* “None” can take both singular and plural verbs depending on meaning.

---

## Singular Use of None

### Examples

* None is well.
* None is waiting for you.
* None is sitting in the class.
* None was available.

---

## Plural Use of None

### Examples

* None are waiting for you.
* None are sitting in the class.
* None were ready.
* None have completed the work.

---

# 4. More → ज्यादा

### Rule

* Use **singular verb** for uncountable nouns.
* Use **plural verb** for plural countable nouns.

---

## Plural Use of More

### Examples

* More people are coming.
* More are dancing and some are sitting.
* More students have joined.
* More workers were needed.

---

## Singular Use of More

### Examples

* More water is required.
* More money is saved for my study.
* More milk was needed.
* More information is available.

---
